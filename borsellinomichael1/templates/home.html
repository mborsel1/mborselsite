ABOUT ME

Urban Planning Student

Michael Borsellino is currently pursuing his doctorate in urban planning at the University of Michigan – Ann Arbor. He is passionate about affordable housing and community growth. Using an urban theory framework, his dissertation will explore these topics through a critique of supply-side housing economics. He is also interested in public financing of stadiums and large events. Michael defected to UM from Big Ten rival Rutgers University where he earned degrees in City & Regional Planning (MCRP, 2017) and Geography (BA, 2015). While at Rutgers, Michael actively engaged with local community children as a community mentor with the Pilot Me program.


Michael is an avid runner and he also enjoys gardening and cooking. He has recently been learning how to program in python and HTML – this site is one of his ongoing projects. When he is too tired to do those things, he reads. Recently, he has been interested in constitutional case law, conflict in the Middle East, political economic theory, and dystopian fiction. You can follow along with some of what he has been reading by checking out his Twitter.
