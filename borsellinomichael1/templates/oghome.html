ABOUT ME

Urban Planning Student

Michael Borsellino is currently pursuing his doctorate in urban planning at the University of Michigan – Ann Arbor. He is passionate about affordable housing and community growth. Using an urban theory framework, his dissertation will explore these topics through a critique of supply-side housing economics. Michael defected to UM from Big Ten rival Rutgers University where he earned degrees in City & Regional Planning (MCRP, 2017) and Geography (BA, 2015). While at Rutgers, Michael actively engaged with local community children as a community mentor with the Pilot Me program.


In his free time (ha!), Michael engages in too many activities. He is an avid runner and he also enjoys gardening and cooking. Michael has recently been learning how to program in python and HTML – this site is one of his ongoing projects. When he is too tired to do those things, he reads. Recently, he has been reading constitutional case law, conflict in the Middle East, political economic theory, and dystopian fiction. Finally, Michael loves stimulating discussions on any subject. Two topics that he has been known to discuss at length are potatoes and the merits of soup.
